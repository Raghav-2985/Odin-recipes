The Indian Paneer Lababdar

  1. Ingredients:
      2. For the Gravy Base
      • Tomatoes: 2 cups (approx. 3 medium), roughly chopped
      • Cashews: 10-15 whole cashews
      • Aromatics: 1 inch ginger, 2-3 cloves garlic
      • Whole Spices: 1-2 dried red chilies (Kashmiri for color/mild heat), 2 cloves, 1 green cardamom pod, 1 inch cinnamon stick
      • Water: 1 cup, for boiling
      For the Curry
      • Paneer: 200g cut into cubes, 50g grated.
      • Onion: ½ cup finely chopped (approx. 1 medium).
      • Fats:2 tbsp oil, 1 tbsp butter or ghee.
      • Powdered Spices: ½ tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp red chili powder, ¼ tsp turmeric powder, ¼ tsp garam masala.
      • Finishing Touches: 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed; 2-3 tbsp fresh cream; 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves; salt and sugar to taste.
      • Optional: 1 bay leaf for tempering.

  2. Instructions
    • Prepare the Base: In a saucepan, combine the tomatoes, cashews, ginger, garlic, whole spices, and 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for 10 minutes until the tomatoes are soft.
    • Cool and Blend:Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool completely. Transfer everything (including any leftover liquid) to a blender jar and blend into a very smooth puree. Set aside.
    • Sauté the Onions: In a separate pan or kadai, heat the oil and butter/ghee over medium heat. Add the bay leaf (if using) and then the finely chopped onions. Sauté until the onions are light golden brown.
    • Cook the Paste:Pour the blended tomato-cashew paste into the pan. Add the powdered spices (cumin, coriander, red chili,turmeric). Stir well and cook the masala on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and you see oil/fat separating from the sides (about 10-15 minutes).
    • Simmer the Gravy: Add about ¾ to 1 cup of water to achieve the desired gravy consistency and bring it to a simmer. Season with salt and sugar to balance the flavors.
    • Add Paneer and Finish: Gently add the paneer cubes and the grated paneer to the gravy. Mix well. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it in, along with the garam masala powder. Turn off the heat.
    • Final Touches: Stir in the fresh cream and garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Cover and let it rest for 5 minutes for the paneer to absorb the flavors before serving.
